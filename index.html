International Faculty

Dr. Sameer Mehta

Dr. Sameer Mehta is an Interventional Cardiologist based in Miami, Florida, USA.
Dr. Mehta did his Internal Medicine, Cardiology and Interventional Cardiology training in New York and an MBA from the University of Miami.
Dr. Mehta has been Chief of Interventional Cardiology and President of the American Heart Association in Miami. He is also the President of the Indian Society of Interventional Cardiologists. Dr. Mehta has more than 200 scientific publications and he has taught angioplasty, atherectomy and stenting in more than 50 countries.
Dr. Mehta is a world-expert in Primary PCI the urgent procedure for stenting and treating acute MI. Dr. Mehta probably has the world’s largest experience in D2B STEMI Interventions and he has created STEMI networks in more than 20 countries. Dr. Mehta is the Course Director of Lumen Global Symposium, a premier AMI subject centric Annual meeting. Dr. Mehta is also the author of three Textbooks of STEMI Interventions.

Dr.Srihari S. Naidu

Dr.Srihari S. Naidu is Director at Cardiac Catheterization Laboratory, Interventional Cardiology Fellowship Program and Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy Treatment Center and also Associate Professor of Medicine – SUNY Stony Brook School of Medicine Winthrop University Hospital, Mineola, New York

National Faculty

Dr.Daggubati Ramesh

Dr. Ramesh Daggubati is board-certified in Cardiovascular Diseases and Interventional Cardiology. He is a Clinical Professor at Brody School of Medicine, East Carolina University and serves as the Director of the Interventional Cardiology Fellowship Program in the Department of Cardiovascular Sciences. In addition, he leads as the Director of the Cardiac Catheterization Laboratories, Co-Director of the Structural Heart Disease Service Line at East Carolina Heart Institute at Vidant Medical Center. He earned his medical degree from Kasturba Medical College in India. His post-graduate training continued at Jamaica Hospital Medical Center as an Internal Medicine Resident, Winthrop University Hospital as a Cardiology Fellow, and Yale University-Bridgeport Hospital as an Interventional Cardiology Fellow.
Dr. Daggubati’s clinical interests focus on complex coronary interventions and percutaneous mitral and aortic valve disease management. He has published in multiple academic journals and lectures nationally and internationally. He has served on sponsored various industry advisory boards, and is currently a member of the SCAI Education Committee Executive Council, SCAI.org Editorial Advisory Board, and the SCAI International Program Committee. He is one of the course directors for C3 conference.

Dr.Matheen A. Khuddus

Matheen A. Khuddus, M.D. is a native of South Florida and is a graduate of the University of Florida College of Medicine, Junior Honors Medical Program. He completed his Residency in Internal Medicine as well as his Fellowship training in Cardiology and Interventional Cardiology at the University of Florida. He joined The Cardiac & Vascular Institute in 2008 and currently serves as the Medical Director and the Director of Cardiovascular Research.
Dr. Khuddus maintains several professional associations including designations as a Fellow of the American College of Cardiology and the Society of Cardiovascular Interventions and Angiography. He is certified by the American Board of Internal Medicine in Cardiovascular Medicine, Interventional Cardiology, and Internal Medicine. He is also board certified in Nuclear Cardiology and is an American Society of Hypertension Certified Specialist in Clinical Hypertension.
